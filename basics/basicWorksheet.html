1 A basic use case

We use\begin{definition} for definitions and \begin{question} for questions. Since Ximera provides immediate feedback, we suggest following definitions like this one by a quick question. Here’s an example:

The absolute value of a real number \(a\), denoted by \(|a|\), is \[ |a| = \begin{cases} a & \text {if $a \geq 0$} \\ -a & \text {if $a<0$.} \end{cases} \]

Now students can check their understanding:

Evaluate the following:
(a)
\(|2-5| = \answer {3}\)
(b)
\(|5-2| = \answer {3}\)
(c)
\(|5-\sqrt {2}| = \answer {3.58578643763}\)
(d)
\(|5-\sqrt {2}| = \answer {5-\sqrt {2}}\)

2 A paradox

Here’s something fun

\(0=1\) Let \(x=y\) and write \begin{align} x^2 & = xy \\ x^2 - y^2 & = xy - y^2 \\ (x-y)(x+y) & = (x-y)y \\ (x+y) & = y \\ 2y & = y \\ 2 & =1. \end{align}

Where is the mistake in the work above? \[ \text {Between line }\answer {3} \text { and line }\answer {4}. \]

3 Basic exercises

After that, you might want to have some exercises:

Let \(x\) be the number of people out of \(100\) that LOVE Ximera.

Find the value of \(x\). \[ x = \answer {100} \]

Maybe you want a problem with one or more parts,

Ximera is so awesome because it feels like:
Doing taxes Writing a book by hand A walk in the park with free ice cream Solving a puzzle blindfolded
Why is Ximera the best thing since the chalkboard?
It turns LaTeX into online materials It boosts student engagement It makes coffee and hugs you Its open-source and free

It ends with an “arlie.”
